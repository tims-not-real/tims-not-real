I'm a Vienna-based complex systems scientist. My background is in mathematical physics and I work on complex systems theory at CSH, Vienna as a Ph.D. candidate.

I'm interested in understanding the deep roots to societal problems by looking at governance, ideology, and social networks, through a complex systems lens. These happen to correspond to the management, the comprising agents, and the systemic properties of complex systems, respectively.

Before CSH I was a visiting scholar at GSAIS, Kyoto University (a.k.a. 思修館) studying the entropy of complex systems under the supervision of Prof. Liang Zhao. Prior to that I completed my honours degree at Swinburne University, Melbourne studying novel self-avoiding walk models under supervision by Prof. Nathan Clisby.

Feel welcomed to reach out to me to talk about any ideas, requests, or questions you might have. I'm always happy to make good ideas happen. Contact: mail@timbooker.net.
