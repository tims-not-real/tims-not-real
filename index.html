Tim Booker

Welcome to my page. This is a space for you to learn about my research, thoughts, and character.

I hold the opinion that there are deep roots to the problem of human society. To understand these roots, I'm interested in the threads of governance, ideology, and social networks. These corespond nicely to the management, the comprising agents, and systemic properties of complex systems, respectively.

My background is in mathematical physics and I currently work on complex systems theory at CSH, Vienna as a Ph.D. candidate.

Feel welcomed to reach out to me to talk about any ideas, requests, or questions you might have. I'm always happy to ideate together and make good ideas happen. Contact: mail@timbooker.net.

Previously I was a visiting scholar at GSAIS, Kyoto University (a.k.a. 思修館) studying the entropy of complex systems under the supervision of Prof. Liang Zhao. I completed my honours degree at Swinburne University, Melbourne, studying novel self-avoiding walk models under supervision by Prof. Nathan Clisby.
